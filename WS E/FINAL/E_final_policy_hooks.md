# Workstream E — Policy Hooks (DRAFT for ratification)

**Status:** proposed by the Data Lead from the validated final metrics. **Risk Consultant + EM confirm the wording before Gate-3 sign-off** — each is a real lending lever feeding Workstream I.

Final 6-segment scheme (product × risk-grade). Default = seasoned (MOB ≥ 3); value = all loans.

| # | Segment | % book | Default | Mean value | Quadrant | Posture | Policy hook (draft) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | BNPL (grade A–C) | 25.9% | 5.7% | −₹661 | Maintain | Fix economics | Keep for reach/engagement, but redesign pricing & cost-to-serve — value-negative at scale (78% loss-making). |
| 2 | Personal Prime (A–B) | 20.1% | 4.3% | +₹4,578 | Maintain | Nurture & deepen | Hold terms; cross-sell and offer measured limit increases. |
| 3 | Personal Non-prime (C–E) | 24.9% | 10.6% | +₹5,923 | Contain | Price for risk | Risk-based pricing, cap limits/tenure, route to early-warning monitoring (WS F). |
| 4 | SME Prime (A–B) | 9.2% | 3.1% | +₹32,204 | Grow | Expand aggressively | Lowest risk, high value — expand limits, prioritise acquisition, speed approvals. |
| 5 | SME Non-prime (C–E) | 11.0% | 8.1% | +₹40,955 | Grow (guardrails) | Grow selectively | Highest value, elevated risk — grow with risk-based pricing, tighter underwriting/collateral. |
| 6 | Subprime BNPL (D–E) | 8.9% | **44.1%** | −₹1,642 | Exit | Halt & re-underwrite | 44% default and value-destroying — halt BNPL originations at grade D–E, tighten the cutoff, wind down the existing book. |

**Two points for the ratification discussion:**
- Segment *value* is driven by product and ticket size, **not** creditworthiness (value uncorrelated with the latent-risk field). "Grow" segments 4 & 5 is a product/economics call, grown *with* risk guardrails — not a low-risk call.
- The behavioural-distress cohort (catastrophic default, defined by payment bounces/shocks) is **not** one of these static segments — it is carried to Workstream F as an early-warning input.
